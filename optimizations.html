Shining Star



Optimizations

Initially building the application, I instantiated a sphere for each star that had a hip number or was within 25 light years from the sun. Due to the high number of game objects, the frames per second was very low. To increase the frame rate, I moved from using spheres to cubes to quads, which performed significantly better. Since quads were used instantiate the stars in the application, billboarding was implemented to ensure that the stars always faced the camera. Although billboarding caused the frame rates to drop, my application still ran at ~30fps while showing ~100,000 stars and constellations.

The biggest optimization issue I ran into was getting the application to run at an acceptable frame rate when movement was introduced. With ~100,000 stars and movement of all the stars, the frame rate dropped to ~4fps. This low frame rate was unacceptable and I spent a few days researching how to increase the fps without having to decrease the number of stars shown. I came across a few solutions on YouTube but they were quite advanced for my level and I couldn't implement them in time. Unfortunately, I did have to decrease the number of stars shown in order to achieve an acceptable frame rate when the stars are moving. The final application instantiates ~20,000 stars but runs at ~100fps when stars are not moving and ~35fps when stars are moving. With that said, I still have a lot to learn regarding how to optimize applications with a lot of data.
